Final assessment

This blueprint is already well thought out and technically feasible. Its strongest qualities are the clear ministry vision, the distinctive "Antigravity" brand identity, the RAG-based architecture, and the emphasis on ethical safeguards.

If I were preparing this for investors, church leadership, or a software development team, I would expand it from a 4-page concept document into a 40–60 page Product Requirements Document (PRD) covering:

Complete feature specifications
User personas and user journeys
UI/UX wireframes
Database schema
API architecture
AI/RAG pipeline design
Security and compliance
Admin portal requirements
Monetization strategy
Development roadmap (MVP → Version 2 → Version 3)
Deployment and maintenance plan

With that level of documentation, a development team could move directly into implementation with far fewer ambiguities. Given your background in software development and your long-term interest in AI-powered ministry products, I believe that investment in detailed planning would pay off significantly during development.
